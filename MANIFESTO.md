# Dayan AI Workshop Manifesto

AI work should not be trusted because it sounds confident.

It should be trusted because the team can answer five questions:

1. What context did the AI use?
2. What actions was it allowed to take?
3. What was blocked or approval-gated?
4. How was the output verified?
5. Where is the handoff for the next person or agent?

## The Belief

The next useful layer of AI is not a bigger prompt. It is a better workshop:

- reusable skills instead of one-off instructions;
- permission redlines instead of blind autonomy;
- verifiers instead of vibes;
- handoffs instead of lost context;
- public templates instead of private magic.

## The Rule

If an AI workflow cannot be replayed, checked, or handed off, it is still a demo.

## The Promise

Dayan AI Workshop publishes the boring-looking parts that make AI work usable in real teams:

- acceptance contracts;
- permission matrices;
- handoff formats;
- governance maps;
- practical comparison pages;
- copyable templates.

Not every AI task needs heavy process. But when the work touches customers, code, money, data, or reputation, the workflow needs a spine.
